Key Findings

  • "Once In A Lifetime" is extraordinarily well-named - The probability appears to be less than 1 in 700 million
  • Even getting close is rare - Only ~0.18% of games achieve 2 stacks (minimum observed)
  • Most games end with 4-5 stacks - 78% of all simulated games
  • Computational intensity required - Hundreds of millions of simulations needed for statistical significance
  • Julia provides 7x performance boost - Essential for large-scale probability estimation

Game Rules

  1. Use all 52 cards from a standard deck
  2. Draw cards one by one, placing them in stacks
  3. Cards match if they have the same rank OR same suit
  4. When cards match, they merge into stacks (within 3-stack range)
  5. Goal: Get all cards into one stack (the "Once In A Lifetime" win condition)

This analysis demonstrates that some solitaire games are truly "once in a lifetime" experiences.
The mathematical rarity makes each attempt both hopeful and humbling.
